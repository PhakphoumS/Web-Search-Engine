<h3>Andrea Rottigni Thesis Presentation: &ldquo;Serious Games for Virtual Rehabilitation in a Large Scale Virtual Reality Environment&rdquo;</h3>
<div id='entry_image_large'><img style='left:1px;' src='/resizedImages/1280x720-rehabjim.png' alt='' title='Andrea Rottigni Thesis Presentation: &ldquo;Serious Games for Virtual Rehabilitation in a Large Scale Virtual Reality Environment&rdquo;' width='638' height='360'></div>
<span class='smallText'>User interactis with &ldquo;RehabJim&rdquo; rehabilitation application - A. Rottigni, UIC/EVL</span><br><br>
<div class='entry_content'>
	<strong>Participants:</strong> <a href='/entry.php?id=2290'>Andrea Rottigni</a>, <a href='/entry.php?id=2156'>Marco Cavallo</a>, Andrea Rottigni<br><br>
<!-- Begin Entry Description -->
Wednesday, May 6, 2016, 11:00 AM<br>
Room 1000, Science & Engineering Office Building, UIC<br><br>
UIC Computer Science and EVL exchange student from the Politecnico di Milano in Italy, Andrea Rottigni presents his thesis research that explores the use of Virtual Reality for upper-limb and walking rehabilitation.<br><br>
&ldquo;RehabJim&rdquo; is a serious game designed for post-stroke patients to help with upper-limb rehabilitation using the CAVE2&trade; Hybrid Reality Environment. The application can be run in two modes - first and third person perspectives to allow domain scientists to better understand, which mode might be most effective in patient rehabilitation. A small user study was conducted to evaluate the efficacy of each perspective mode.<br><br>
&ldquo;CAVEChef&rdquo;, another serious game developed for the CAVE2 to assist post-stroke patients in walking rehabilitation tasks.  &ldquo;CAVEChef&rdquo; uses a third person perspective.<br><br>
