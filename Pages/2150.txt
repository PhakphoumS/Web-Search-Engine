          							<h1 class="title">Rachel A. Gordon, PhD</h1>
			          
	        
          



<div style="position: relative; width: 63%; float: left;">

      <div class="field-field-researcher-photo">
      <img src="https://www.ihrp.uic.edu/files/2018.Gordon.Rachel25-150x200.jpg" />

  <h2>
      Professor of Sociology  </h2>

  <p>
    <p>Dr. Rachel A. Gordon is a professor of sociology,&nbsp;a Faculty Fellow of the Honors College, and a <a href="/content/about-our-fellows-affiliates">Fellow of the Institute for Health Research and Policy</a> at the University of Illinois at Chicago. She is a faculty member of the Institute of Government and Public Affairs (IGPA) of the University of Illinois.</p>
<p>Dr. Gordon&#39;s research broadly aims to measure and model the contexts of the lives of children and families, often using longitudinal data sets. She has examined numerous contextual and social factors that affect children and families, including the use of child care and preschool quality measures for high-stakes policy purposes, the health outcomes of child care and maternal employment, the implications of teenagers&#39; looks for their social and academic achievement, the association between community context and child well-being, the relationships between youth gang participation and delinquency, the causes and consequences of grandmother co-residential support for young mothers, and the evaluation of an innovative job program for young couples.</p>
<p>Dr. Gordon was named UIC Distinguished Researcher of 2018 for her record of outstanding achievement in the social sciences. <a href="https://today.uic.edu/2018-researcher-of-the-year-rachel-gordon">Learn more.</a></p>
<p>Since 2008, she has been the principal investigator of research on child care services and child development funded by the USDA Economic Research Service, the Institute of Education Services, the National Institutes of Health, and the National Science Foundation. She also has conducted research through the <a href="https://igpa.uillinois.edu/policy-initiatives/early-investments">IGPA Early Investments Initiative</a>.</p>
<p>Dr. Gordon is a member of the <a href="https://s-r-a.org/index.php/about-sra/governance-committees/">Society for Research on Adolescence</a> (SRA) Social Policy Committee and co-chairs the SRA&#39;s 2020 Biennial Meeting in San Diego.</p>
<p>Dr. Gordon was trained as pre-doctoral student with funding by the National Institute on Child Health and Human Development, received her doctorate in public policy from the University of Chicago, and was a post-doctoral research scientist for two years at the NORC/University of Chicago Sloan Center on Parents, Children and Work.</p>
  </p>
