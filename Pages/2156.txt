  <h2>
      Director, Data Management Core  </h2>

  <p>
    <p>Dr. Kathleen Diviak is the director of the <a href="/center/data-management-core-all-UIC-scientists">Data Management Core</a> of the Institute for Health Research and Policy of the University of Illinois at Chicago and an <a href="/content/about-our-fellows-affiliates">IHRP Fellow</a>. A licensed clinical psychologist, Dr. Diviak has participated on multidisciplinary tobacco control research teams for over 20 years.<br />
	<br />
	Dr. Diviak&#39;s expertise is in adolescent and young adult tobacco use, ecological momentary assessment (EMA) study design and implementation, and recruitment and retention in longitudinal studies of health behavior.</p>
<p>As the investigator responsible for the recruitment and retention the cohort of nearly 1,300 study participants in the NCI-funded longitudinal study, Social-Emotional Contexts of Adolescent and Young Adult Smoking Patterns, Dr. Diviak has over a decade of experience carrying out the work of longitudinal studies of health behavior -- in laboratory, clinic, and community settings -- with demanding study protocols, ranging from week-long EMA to videotaped home observations of family discussions. She is greatly skilled in developing recruitment strategies as well as partnering with community members, organizations, and businesses for longitudinal research and health interventions.</p>
<p>Dr. Diviak earned her doctorate in clinical psychology at the University of Illinois at Chicago, where she wrote her dissertation on smoking cessation.</p>
  </p>
