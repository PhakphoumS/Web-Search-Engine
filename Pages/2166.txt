<div style="position: relative; width: 63%; float: left;">

  <h2>
      Professor of Community Health Sciences  </h2>

  <p>
    <p>Dr. Naoko Muramatsu is a professor in the <a href="https://publichealth.uic.edu/profiles/naoko-muramatsu/">Division of Community Health Sciences</a> in the UIC School of Public Health and a faculty affiliate in the Department of Sociology in the UIC College of Liberal Arts and Sciences. She conducts her research at the Institute for Health Research and Policy, where she is recognized as a <a href="/content/about-our-fellows-affiliates">Fellow</a>.</p>
<p>Dr. Muramatsu conducts research that improves the quality of care and well-being of diverse aging populations locally and globally. Her research investigates how place impacts aging, health, work, and caregiving experiences. Guided by life course perspectives, Dr. Muramatsu advances health equity by identifying critical factors that lead to disparities in stressors, resources, and health over the life course and by intervening on those factors. She uses multilevel longitudinal analysis and mixed methods to understand and intervene upon the individual, organizational, and system-level factors that impact aging populations and the quality of health care systems.</p>
<p>As principal investigator of multiple studies funded by the National Institute on Aging, Dr. Muramatsu has examined how state-, community-, and individual-level factors influence the well-being of older adults. Her current research develops cost-effective, sustainable community-based health promotion programs in partnership with older adults, caregivers, community-based organizations, and multidisciplinary research teams.</p>
<p>A Fellow of <a href="https://www.geron.org/press-room/press-releases/2015-press-releases/490-the-gerontological-society-of-america-selects-2015-fellows">The Gerontological Society of America</a>, Dr. Muramatsu serves on the jury of the <a href="http://rymanprize.com/">Ryman Prize</a> as well as on the editorial boards of <a href="http://gerontologist.oxfordjournals.org/">The Gerontologist</a>. and the <a href="https://www.tandfonline.com/toc/wasp20/current">Journal of Aging &amp; Social Policy</a>.</p>
  </p>

  <p>
    <h2>Research Interests</h2>
  </p>
  <p>
    <p>Aging; long-term care; caregiving; workplace health; access to and quality of health services; well-being and quality of life; global aging, technology, and health.</p>
  </p>
