<div style="position: relative; width: 63%; float: left;">

  <h2>
      Associate Professor, Kinesiology and Nutrition  </h2>

  <p>
    <p>Dr. David X. Marquez is an associate professor of kinesiology in the UIC College of Applied Health Sciences. In addition to his independent research conducted in his home department, Dr. Marquez collaborates in multidisiplinary research at IHRP, which recognizes him as a <a href="/content/about-our-fellows-affiliates">Fellow</a>.</p>
<p>Dr. Marquez specializes in exercise psychology and behavioral medicine. He currently chairs the Minority Health and Research Special Interest Group of the <a href="http://www.acsm.org">American College of Sports Medicine</a> and is an advisor in the <a href="http://www.ahs.uic.edu/uaha/">Urban Allied Health Academy</a> at UIC.</p>
<p>Dr. Marquez obtained his bachelor of science degree in psychology from Loyola University Chicago (1997) and his master&#39;s (2000) and doctoral (2004) degrees in kinesiology from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign. From 2004 to 2007, he was an assistant professor in the Departments of Kinesiology and Public Health at the University of Massachusetts, Amherst.</p>
  </p>

  <p>
    <h2>Research Interests</h2>
  </p>
  <p>
    <p>Dr. Marquez&rsquo;s research focuses on disparities in physical activity and disease/disability among Latinos. He is interested in using physical activity interventions towards the prevention of disability and the maintenance of cognitive function and quality of life in older Latino adults. Moreover, he is interested in the interaction between the physical/cultural environment and behavior among underserved populations.</p>
  </p>
