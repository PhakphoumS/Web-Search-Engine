<p><strong><font color="black">ChicagoCHEC Seminar Series</font></strong></p>
<h2>
	Crafting Your NIH Biosketch: Strengthening Your Biosketch To Better Highlight Goals and Achievements For Grants</h2>
<h3>
	Caryn Peterson, PhD, MS</h3>
<p>Research Assistant Professor of Epidemiology<br />
	Curriculum Director of UIC Cancer Center Education and Career Development Program&nbsp;<br />
	Department of Edidemiology and Biostatics, University of Illinois at Chicago</p>
<h3>
	Wednesday, May 27, 2020<br />
	Noon to 1 p.m.<br />
	Online<br />
	&nbsp;</h3>
<p><a href="https://publichealth.uic.edu/profiles/caryn-peterson/">Dr. Caryn E. Peterson</a> <span class="print-footnote">[3]</span> is a Research Assistant Professor of Epidemiology, University of Illinois at Chicago, School of Public Health and Co-Director, Cancer Education &amp; Career Development Program, Institute of Health Research and Policy.&nbsp;</p>
<p>During her webinar, Dr. Peterson will review what an NIH biosketch is, how it can be used to best highlight career goals and achievements, and provide in-depth analysis on how to strengthen your own.&nbsp;</p>
<p>The Chicago Cancer Health Equity Collaborative (ChicagoCHEC) Seminar Series is a free webinar series. Registration for the webinar is required.</p>
<p><br />
	<strong>Questions?</strong></p>
<p>For additional details on how to join the webinar, send any questions and concerns to <a href="mailto:info@chicagochec.org">info@chicagochec.org</a> <span class="print-footnote">[4]</span>.</p>
