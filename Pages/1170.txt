<h3>Interactive Exploration and Tracking of Viscous Fingers in Large-Scale Ensemble Simulations</h3>
<div id='entry_image_large'><img style='left:7px;' src='/resizedImages/1280x720-viscousfingers_burks_sugiyama.png' alt='' title='Interactive Exploration and Tracking of Viscous Fingers in Large-Scale Ensemble Simulations' width='625' height='360'></div>
<span class='smallText'>Screen Shot of Viscous Fingers Visualization Application - Andrew Burks, EVL/UIC</span><br><br>
<div class='entry_content'>
	<strong>Researchers:</strong> <a href='/entry.php?id=2231'>Andrew Burks</a>, <a href='/entry.php?id=2230'>Cassiano Sugiyama</a>, <a href='/entry.php?id=2052'>G. Elisabeta Marai</a>, <a href='/entry.php?id=2189'>Timothy Luciani</a><br><br>
	<strong>Funding:</strong> NSF CAREER Award Research Experience Undergraduate Program<br><br>
<!-- Begin Entry Description -->
The visualization of aleatoric uncertainty in ensemble simulations - i.e. the study of high concentrations in mixtures - provides many challenges due to the size and dimensionality of the data sets. EVL researchers have developed a web-based application designed to locate and track areas of high concentration in salt-solution simulations.<br><br>
The goal is to learn more about the behavior of Viscous Fingers, or contiguous areas of high concentration of salt within a cylinder of pure water.  The application allows viewers to explore 3-D ensemble simulation data using both 2-D vertical slices of the cylindrical data to get a more detailed view of the data in a specific time step, as well as a feature tracking merge tree to better understand the evolution of these Viscous Fingers over the course of a simulation run.<br><br>
