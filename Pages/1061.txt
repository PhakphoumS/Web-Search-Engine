<div style="position: relative; width: 63%; float: left;">

  <h2>
      IHRP Associate Director and Professor of Community Health Sciences  </h2>

  <p>
    <p>David L. DuBois, PhD, is a professor in the Division of <a href="https://publichealth.uic.edu/profiles/david-dubois/">Community Health Sciences</a> and the associate dean of research in the School of Public Health at the University of Illinois at Chicago. He is associate director of the Institute for Health Research and Policy and an <a href="/content/about-our-fellows-affiliates">IHRP Fellow</a>.</p>
<p>Dr. DuBois&#39;s research examines the contribution of protective factors, particularly self-esteem and mentoring relationships, to resilience and holistic positive development and on translating knowledge in this area to the design of effective youth programs. He has authored numerous peer-reviewed studies on these topics, including two widely-cited meta-analyses of the effectiveness of youth mentoring programs.</p>
<p>He also is the lead editor of the <a href="https://us.sagepub.com/en-us/nam/handbook-of-youth-mentoring/book234516">Handbook of Youth Mentoring</a> (Sage Publications) and co-author of <a href="http://www.cambridge.org/US/academic/subjects/psychology/developmental-psychology/after-school-centers-and-youth-development-case-studies-success-and-failure">After-School Centers and Youth Development: Case Studies of Success and Failure</a> (Cambridge University Press). Both books received awards for their social policy impact from the <a href="http://www.s-r-a.org/">Society of Research on Adolescence</a>.</p>
<p>Dr. DuBois&#39;s research has been funded by the National Institutes of Health, Office of Juvenile Justice and Delinquency Prevention, DHHS Office of Minority Health, and the Institute of Education Sciences. He is a Fellow of the American Psychological Association and Society for Community Research and Action as well as a past Distinguished Fellow of the William T. Grant Foundation. He received his doctorate in clinical-community psychology from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign.</p>
