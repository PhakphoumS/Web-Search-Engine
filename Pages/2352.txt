  <h3>
	Thursday, April 24, 2020<br />
	10:30 &ndash; 12:30p.m.</h3>
<p>Virtual Event</p>
<p>This annual student-run event at the School of Public Health will focus on understanding the health equity and social justice dimensions of COVID-19.</p>
<p>RSVP Required. Register online at&nbsp;<a href="http://go.uic.edu/JACSWHonigLecture">go.uic.edu/JACSWHonigLecture</a> <span class="print-footnote">[2]</span>.</p>
<p>Registered attendees will receive the webinar link via email.</p>
<h3>
	Panelists:</h3>
<h2>
	Dr. Georges Benjamin</h2>
<p>Executive Director<br />
	American Public Health Association</p>
<p><a href="https://apha.org/about-apha/executive-board-and-staff/apha-staff/georges-c-benjamin-md">Dr. Georges Benjamin</a> <span class="print-footnote">[3]</span> is known as one of the nation&rsquo;s most influential physician leaders because he speaks passionately and eloquently about the health issues having the most impact on our nation today.</p>
<h2>
	Dr. Linda Rae Murray</h2>
<p>Adjunct Assistant Professor of Health Policy and Administration<br />
	UIC School of Public Health</p>
<p><a href="https://www.apha.org/about-apha/executive-board-and-staff/apha-executive-board/linda-rae-murray">Dr. Linda Rae Murray</a> <span class="print-footnote">[4]</span> is the former chief medical officer for the Cook County Department of Public Health. She has spent her career serving the medically under served.</p>
<h2>
	Joseph Harrington</h2>
<p>President and CEO<br />
	The Mojo Group</p>
<p>A native Chicagoan,<a href="https://www.linkedin.com/in/joseph-harrington-41a89938/"> Joseph Harrington</a> <span class="print-footnote">[5]</span> has worked in the fields of education, social services, mental health, healthcare and public health.</p>
<h2>
	Aida Luz Giachello, PhD</h2>
<p>Research Professor of Preventive Medicine<br />
	Northwestern University Feinberg School of Medicine</p>
<p><a href="https://www.feinberg.northwestern.edu/faculty-profiles/az/profile.html?xid=21400">Aida Luz Giachello</a> <span class="print-footnote">[6]</span>&#39;s research focuses on social and racial health disparities, particularly among Hispanics/Latinos and African Americans, chronic diseases, women&#39;s health, and tobacco control and prevention.</p>
<h3>
	Questions?</h3>
<p>Contact Linda Pineda at <a href="mailto:lcpineda@uic.edu">lcpineda@uic.edu</a> <span class="print-footnote">[7]</span>.</p>
