  <h2>
      Professor of Health Policy and Administration  </h2>

  <p>
    <p>Dr. Jamie F. Chriqui is a professor in the <a href="https://publichealth.uic.edu/profiles/jamie-chriqui/">Division of Health Policy and Administration</a> in the UIC School of Public Health and a <a href="/content/about-our-fellows-affiliates">Fellow</a> at the Institute for Health Research and Policy, where co-directs the <a href="/center/health-policy-center">Health Policy Center</a> and conducts her research.</p>
<h3>
	Dr. Chriqui has over 25 years&#39; experience conducting public health policy research, evaluation, and analysis, with an emphasis on obesity, substance abuse, tobacco control, and other chronic disease-related policy issues.</h3>
<p>Dr. Chriqui&#39;s research interests focus on examining the impact of law and policy on practices, community environments, and individual behaviors/attitudes. In the <a href="http://www.bridgingthegapresearch.org">Bridging the Gap Research</a> program, which ended in June 2016, she directed all state, local, and school district obesity policy research as well as leading the state tobacco control policy research activities.</p>
<p>She holds an appointment on the <a href="http://www.thecommunityguide.org/about/task-force-members.html">Community Preventive Services Task Force</a> for 2016-2021. She served on the National Academy of Medicine consensus committee charged with <a href="http://www.nationalacademies.org/hmd/Activities/Nutrition/EvaluatingProgressObesityPrevention.aspx">Evaluating Progress of Obesity Prevention Efforts</a>, the IOM committee that wrote <a href="http://www.nationalacademies.org/hmd/Reports/2012/Accelerating-Progress-in-Obesity-Prevention.aspx">Accelerating Progress in Obesity Prevention: Solving the Weight of the Nation</a>, and the IOM committee that <a href="http://nationalacademies.org/hmd/reports/2013/evaluating-obesity-prevention-efforts-a-plan-for-measuring-progress.aspx">developed a plan</a> for measuring progress in that prevention effort.</p>
<p>Dr. Chriqui holds a BA in political science from Barnard College at Columbia University, an MHS in health policy from the Johns Hopkins University School of Hygiene and Public Health, and a PhD in policy sciences (with a health policy concentration) from the University of Maryland, Baltimore County.</p>
<p>The UIC Vice Chancellor for Research named Dr. Chriqui the 2016 <a href="https://today.uic.edu/improving-health-through-public-policy">Distinguished Researcher of the Year for the Social Sciences</a>.</p>
