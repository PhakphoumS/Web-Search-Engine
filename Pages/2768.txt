<h3>Expanding the Porthole: Leveraging Large, High-Resolution Displays in Exploratory Visual Analysis</h3>
<div id='entry_image_large'><img style='left:0;' src='/resizedImages/1280x720-chi14_reda.png' alt='' title='Expanding the Porthole: Leveraging Large, High-Resolution Displays in Exploratory Visual Analysis' width='640' height='347'></div>
<span class='smallText'>The visualization environment employed in the pilot user study - L. Long, EVL</span><br><br>
<div class='entry_content'>
	<strong>Authors:</strong> <a href='/entry.php?id=259'>Reda, K.</a>, Offord, C., <a href='/entry.php?id=10'>Johnson, A.</a>, <a href='/entry.php?id=21'>Leigh, J.</a><br><br>
	<strong>Publication:</strong> <em>Proceedings of CHI&rsquo;14 EA on Human Factors on Computing Systems</em>, Toronto, Ontario, Canada, pp. 2047-2052, ACM<br><br>
	<strong>URL:</strong> <a href='http://dl.acm.org/citation.cfm?doid=2559206.2581202' target='_blank'>http://dl.acm.org/citation.cfm?doid=2559206.2581202</a><br><br>
<!-- Begin Entry Description -->
The scale and complexity of today&rsquo;s datasets frequently overwhelm conventional visualization interfaces, which could negatively impact the quality of the visual analytic activity. In this paper, we investigate the use of Large, High-Resolution displays in exploratory visual analysis scenarios. We argue that the ability to see and interact with more information at once fundamentally affects users&rsquo; analytic behavior, prompting them to explore their data more broadly. This positive effect may also enhance the diversity of questions and hypotheses conceived and explored by users during their analysis.<br><br>

Keywords: Information visualization; visual exploration; visual analytics; large high-resolution displays<br><br>
