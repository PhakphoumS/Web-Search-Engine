                    <h2 id="is-lasuri-restricted-to-certain-majors" class="__title invisible">Is LASURI restricted to certain majors?</h2>
                    </div>

  
  <p>LASURI is open to any undergraduate, degree-seeking student whose major field of study is in the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences.  We encourage students majoring in the humanities, social sciences, natural sciences, and interdisciplinary programs in LAS to apply for the award.</p>

                    </div>

  
  <p>We encourage you to continue your research partnership over several semesters. However, to serve the largest number of students as possible, LASURI funds are limited to two semesters.</p>
<p>Students who have already received a two-semester LASURI award are urged to apply for other scholarships or register for course credit instead. Those who have received one semester may reapply for one additional semester.</p>

                    <h2 id="what-if-i-want-to-work-on-my-own-project-rather-than-a-faculty-members-project" class="__title invisible">What if I want to work on my own project, rather than a faculty member's project?</h2>
                    </div>

  
  <p>The purpose of LASURI is to facilitate productive research partnerships between professors and undergraduates. Mentors and LASURI research assistants work together on projects they have agreed are sound and feasible for the student. This may include brand new projects or an integration of the LASURI research assistant into a faculty member&#8217;s on-going project.  The fundamental requirement is that all project team members meet the basic program expectations of active mentorship, research activity, a public research presentation, and a final summary.</p>

                    </div>

  
  <p>Mentors from all colleges are welcome, though preference may be given to faculty with a LAS affiliation. Possible faculty mentors may include individuals with a clinical, emeritus, or research associate appointments. Advanced graduate students are not eligible to formally serve as faculty mentors.</p>
<p>If a faculty member has already agreed to serve as mentor for your project, great! Once you enter their contact information in the LASURI student application, the faculty member will receive an email prompting them to complete the mentor portion of the application. Be sure to give them plenty of time before the application deadline to do this; you&#8217;ll be able to see in SnAP when the mentor&#8217;s portion has been submitted.</p>

                    </div>

  
  <p>Full-time enrollment is required to receive LASURI funding. However, if you will be a full-time student during one semester next year, you can apply for only that semester. Most projects approved for LASURI funding carry over two semesters, but the committee will entertain proposals for single-semester projects. Be sure to discuss this with your faculty mentor so you can design a project feasible for this time frame.</p>
<p>If you originally apply for and receive two semesters of funding and later find out that you won&#8217;t be available for the full year, let us know right away. You&#8217;re able to receive funding only for the semester in which you are on campus, enrolled full-time, and participating in LASURI.</p>

                    <h2 id="can-i-work-with-multiple-lasuri-students-either-on-the-same-project-or-on-multiple-projects" class="__title invisible">Can I work with multiple LASURI students, either on the same project or on multiple projects?</h2>
                    </div>

  
  <p>If you are willing to mentor more than one student, you may support more than one application. However, you will be asked to rank these applications and priority may be given to the student(s) you have ranked highest. In general, no more than two student applicants will be approved per faculty mentor.</p>

                    <h2 id="what-does-it-mean-that-my-student-has-been-named-as-an-alternate" class="__title invisible">What does it mean that my student has been named as an alternate?</h2>
                    </div>

  
  <p>The selection committee identifies and ranks alternates to be funded if another participant withdraws from LASURI.  There is no guarantee that an alternate will be offered funding, but it remains a possibility, and being named an alternate should be seen as an acknowledgement of the research proposal&#8217;s strength.</p>
<p>On occasion, the committee will decide to offer one semester of funding to certain projects in an effort to allow more students to participate. If the student decides to move forward with a single semester of funding, they should work with the faculty mentor to adapt the project to the new time frame.</p>
