          							<h1 class="title">Angela Odoms-Young, PhD</h1>
			          
	        
          



<div style="position: relative; width: 63%; float: left;">

      <div class="field-field-researcher-photo">

  <h2>
      Associate Professor, Kinesiology and Nutrition  </h2>

  <p>
    <p>Dr. Angela Odoms-Young is an associate professor in the Department of Kinesiology and Nutrition in the UIC College of Applied Health Sciences and an <a href="/content/about-our-fellows-affiliates">IHRP Fellow</a>.<br />
	<br />
	Dr. Odoms-Young&rsquo;s research is focused on understanding social, cultural, and environmental determinants of dietary behaviors and diet-related diseases in low-income and minority populations. Her current projects include studies to evaluate the impact of the new WIC food package on dietary intake, weight status, and chronic disease risk in 2-3 year old low-income children and vendor participation; identify strategies to improve program participation and retention among WIC eligible children; evaluate the efficacy of a community-based participatory weight loss intervention in African American women; and examine community engagement approaches to promote food justice. She has contributed to several Institute of Medicine reports, including the <a href="http://iom.nationalacademies.org/Reports/2015/Review-of-WIC-Food-Packages-Interim-Report.aspx">Review of WIC Food Packages: Proposed Framework for Revisions: Interim Report</a>, published in November 2015.</p>
<p>Dr. Odoms-Young serves on the executive committee of the Consortium to Lower Obesity in Chicago Children (<a href="http://www.clocc.net/">CLOCC)</a>, which awarded her the 2015 Katherine Kaufer Christoffel Founder&#39;s Award.</p>
<p>Prior to joining UIC, Dr. Odoms-Young served on the faculty of Northern Illinois University in Public Health and Health Education. She completed a Family Research Consortium Postdoctoral Fellowship examining family processes in diverse populations at the Pennsylvania State University and the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign and a Community Health Scholars Fellowship in community-based participatory research at the University of Michigan School of Public Health.</p>
  </p>

  <p>
    <h2>Research Interests</h2>
  </p>
  <p>
    <p>Dr. Odoms-Young&rsquo;s research is focused on social, cultural, and environmental factors that contribute to inequities in diet and obesity-related health outcomes. Her expertise includes obesity prevention, community-engaged and community-based participatory research, health equity, qualitative and ethnographic methods, and program evaluation/planning.</p>
  </p>
