          							<h1 class="title">Oksana Pugach, PhD</h1>
			          
	        
          



<div style="position: relative; width: 63%; float: left;">

  <h2>
      Biostatistician, IHRP Methodology Research Core  </h2>

  <p>
    <p>Dr. Oksana Pugach is a biostatistician in the Institute&#39;s <a href="/center/methodology-research-core">Methodology Research Core</a> and an <a href="/content/about-our-fellows-affiliates">IHRP Fellow</a>.&nbsp;</p>
<h3>
	Dr. Pugach has provided data management and statistical support to multidisciplinary IHRP research teams since 2005.</h3>
<p>She earned her doctoral degree in biostatistics from the University of Illinois at Chicago in 2012. In her dissertation, Dr. Pugach developed a location-scale mixed-effects model for bivariate continuous outcomes, a relatively new methodology of modeling longitudinal bivariate data with heterogeneous within- and between-subject variances.</p>
<p>Dr. Pugach also holds a master&#39;s degree in computer science and has almost 10 years&#39; experience in data management in public health-related projects.</p>
  </p>

  <p>
    <h2>Research Interests</h2>
  </p>
  <p>
    <p>Bivariate mixed-effects location-scale models with specific application to a <a href="/study/social-emotional-contexts-adolescent-smoking-patterns-2004-2010">longitudinal adolescent smoking study</a>. Also multilevel mixed-effects regression models with survey data.</p>
  </p>
